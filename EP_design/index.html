EXTENDED
PLAY BAR

OVERVIEW

Ok, this was a fun project. Not only did I get to do a complete identity for a brand new bar in Honolulu, but I got to build them a website from scratch as well. They already had the logo, so all I had to do was fill in the blanks. Everything from copy-writing to packaging.

TYPOGRAPHY

For type and such, I really wanted the vibe of a magazine spread. Something you would find in a fashion mag. I ended up using a messy casual script font for the headlines. To contrast the headlines, I used a sophisticated geometric san serif for the body copy. I even threw a third condensed typeface into the website to spice things up. Can you find it?

COLORS

Gold was the color of day. I was going for “sophisticated low brow” so I chose more of a yellowish gold. I supplemented it with an off white and an off black and I think the color palette turned out pretty well.

PATTERNS

Since a big draw of Extended Play Bar is the countless records they spin, I thought it would be great to create a pattern that complimented the shape of a record. Something subtle in the background that would make the other content “pop.” These patterns became instrumental in constructing the menu and other Extended Play items.

LETS BUILD A WEBSITE SHALL WE?

It all starts with some shapes. Big shapes, tiny shapes, smooth shapes, hairy shapes... wut? A literal smörgåsbord of shapes! Well, not literally, but you know what I mean. Ooh, and lets add some content and typography as well. They say that content is king and typography is queen, and we don't skip on royalty here. I know, its not much to look at yet, but we're just getting started!

HOW LO-FI CAN YOU GO

Woo wee, now we get to the fun part. Lets do a lil' lo fidelity mockup. Lets organize everyting and maybe start putting in content. Still no color and still no typography specific to the design, but don't you worry, we'll get there.

A HI FIVE FOR HI-FI

Now we're cooking! Our vision is clear. We're adding color, we're setting type and we're designing our way to the finished website. Shall we program? Shall we code? Shall we develop this thing into something we can surf on the interwebs? Um, yes!

AN EVOLUTION OF SORTS

Look, I know I said our vision was clear... But hey, things change. Things evolve. Sometimes I go down a rabbit hole of fashion magazines and change everything. I'm always learning new things, and why not incorporate those news things into your designs. Is it the right way to do things? Maybe not, but the heart wants what the heart wants... and I think it turned out pretty darn good in the end.
